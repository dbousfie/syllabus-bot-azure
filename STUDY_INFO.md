# Information Letter for Research Participants

**Study Title:** Understanding how undergraduate students use an AI syllabus assistant (WREM Project ID: [Insert ID])

**Principal Investigator:** Dan Bousfield, Department of Political Science, Western University. dbousfie@uwo.ca

### 1. Invitation to participate
You are invited to take part in a research study about how students use an optional AI “syllabus assistant” in this class. Please read this letter before deciding. Taking part is entirely your choice.

### 2. Why this study is being done
The study examines what students ask a syllabus assistant, what those questions reveal about the course and syllabus, and how such tools may support learning, in order to improve teaching and contribute to scholarship on teaching and learning.

### 3. What you will be asked to do
Nothing beyond your normal, optional use of the assistant. When you ask a question, it is sent to Western's secure AI service (Microsoft Azure OpenAI) to generate an answer, and the anonymous question, the answer, and any helpful/not-helpful rating you give are recorded. Your instructor reviews these anonymous questions during the course — they cannot see who asked what — and uses them to improve the syllabus, the assistant, and course announcements. You do not have to use the assistant at all.

### 4. Voluntary participation and withdrawal
Participation is completely voluntary. Because your questions are recorded anonymously and the assistant does not keep a session or account, there is no way to find or remove an individual question after it has been submitted — so please decide before you ask. Your decision has no effect on your grades, your standing in the course, or your relationship with the instructor, who cannot tell which students did or did not take part.

### 5. Risks
Risks are minimal. Please do not type identifying information (such as your name or student number) into the assistant. Your question is sent to Western's secure AI service (Microsoft Azure OpenAI) to produce an answer; no information identifying you is sent with it. Any identifying details inadvertently entered will be removed before analysis.

### 6. Benefits
Because your instructor reviews the anonymous questions during the term and updates the course in response, your questions may help improve the syllabus and materials while you are taking the course. The study may also contribute to knowledge about AI-supported learning.

### 7. Confidentiality and anonymity
Your interactions are collected anonymously; only the question text, the answer, and any rating are recorded — no names, student numbers, IP addresses, or logins. Questions are processed by Western's secure AI service (Microsoft Azure OpenAI, under Western's institutional agreement) to generate answers and are stored in a Western Qualtrics survey. Access is limited to the research team, and results are reported only in aggregate or de-identified form. Data will be retained for a minimum of 7 years after project completion.

### 8. Compensation
There is no compensation for participation.

### 9. Conflict of interest
The researcher is also your course instructor. To protect your free choice, participation is anonymous and voluntary and declining has no academic consequence. Your instructor reviews the questions during the course to improve it, but because they are anonymous, your instructor cannot tell who asked any question or who chose to take part, so this cannot affect your grade or standing.

### 10. Questions
For questions about the study, contact Dan Bousfield at dbousfie@uwo.ca. If you have questions about your rights as a research participant or the conduct of this study, you may contact The Office of Human Research Ethics at 519-661-3036 or ethics@uwo.ca.

### 11. Consent
By using the assistant after reading this notice, you understand that your anonymous questions and answers are recorded, reviewed during the course to improve it, and may be used for research that may be published. No information identifying you is collected. You may keep or print a copy of this letter.
