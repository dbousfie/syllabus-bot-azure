# Research Study Information & Participation Details

## Study Title: Evaluating the Utility of an AI Syllabus Assistant

### Invitation to Participate
You are invited to use the AI Syllabus Assistant to help answer questions about your course syllabus. Participation is entirely voluntary. Your decision to use or not use the tool will have no effect on your grades, standing, or relationship with the instructor.

### Purpose of the Study
This research aims to understand how generative AI can assist students in navigating course expectations and to identify common areas where students seek clarification in course materials.

### What is Involved?
The AI assistant is available within our course Brightspace page. When you type a question, the AI provides an answer based on our syllabus. Your interaction—specifically your question, the AI’s response, and your feedback (thumbs up/down)—is recorded for research purposes.

### Confidentiality and Data Security
* **Anonymity:** Your participation is completely anonymous. The system does not collect your name, student number, or any other identifying information.
* **Privacy Warning:** While the system is designed to protect your privacy, please **do not** type personal or identifying information into the chat box.
* **Data Storage:** Your anonymous interactions will be stored securely in the Principal Investigator's password-protected institutional account for seven years following project completion, in accordance with Western University policy.
* **Data Usage:** Data may be used for research on teaching and learning and may be published in academic journals or presented at conferences.

### Risks and Benefits
There are minimal risks associated with this study. The primary risk is the possibility of the AI generating an inaccurate answer; please always refer to the official syllabus document as the final authority. The benefit is receiving 24/7 assistance with course-related questions.

### Contact Information
If you have questions about this research, please contact the Principal Investigator. If you have questions about your rights as a research participant, contact the Office of Human Research Ethics at 519-661-3036 or ethics@uwo.ca.
